About Me

I am a PhD researcher at Johns Hopkins University within the Greenhouse Gas Research Group. My work sits at the intersection of atmospheric science, Bayesian statistics, and machine learning. I specialize in physics-based inverse modeling to quantify methane emissions—a critical driver of climate change.

Before my doctoral studies, I completed my M.H.S. at Johns Hopkins and my undergraduate degree at George Washington University. My journey is defined by a dual pursuit of academic rigor and elite athletic performance, having competed as a top-tier NCAA tennis player while maintaining Academic All-American status.
