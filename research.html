Arctic Methane Flux Estimation via Geostatistical Inverse Modeling

First Author • EGUsphere Preprint • 2025

Quantifying methane emissions across Arctic-boreal North America using physics-based inverse modeling. Combining aircraft and tower observations with atmospheric transport models (STILT) to estimate regional fluxes and reduce uncertainty in emission budgets.

Problem

  • High uncertainty in Arctic methane budgets
  • Sparse observational constraints across vast regions
  • Need for scalable inference methods
  • Seasonal and spatial pattern validation

Method

  • Geostatistical inverse modeling (GIM)
  • Bayesian inference & optimization
  • STILT transport footprint calculations
  • HPC-scalable computation pipelines
  • Uncertainty quantification frameworks

Impact

  • Reduced regional uncertainty in emission budgets
  • Validated against aircraft + tower observations
  • Reproducible analysis pipeline
  • First-author publication (EGUsphere, 2025)

Global Wetland Methane Emissions from Satellite Observations

Co-Author • JGR Atmospheres • 2025

Spatial and temporal analysis of global wetland methane emissions using satellite observations. Developed methods for estimating emissions during 2019–2020 with improved spatial resolution and temporal coverage.

Model Evaluation & Multi-Model Intercomparison

Research • NASA ABoVE • 2024-2025

Systematic evaluation of 16 process-based methane models against atmospheric observations. Identified regional biases, seasonal patterns, and model improvements for high-latitude North America.

Problem

  • Model intercomparison needed
  • Bias identification required
  • Seasonal pattern validation

Method

  • Multi-model ensemble analysis
  • Bias diagnostics across ecoregions
  • Seasonal attribution analysis
  • Statistical evaluation methods

Impact

  • Reduced inter-model uncertainty
  • Identified improvement areas
  • Conference presentations (AGU, AMS, ABoVE)
