Research

Physics-based inverse modeling and Bayesian inference for methane emissions — with scalable data/ML tools for real-world atmospheric systems.

Arctic methane inversion

Geostatistical inversions combining aircraft/tower observations with transport sensitivity (e.g., STILT) to estimate emissions and uncertainties.

  • Top-down flux estimation
  • Prior covariates + model selection
  • Posterior uncertainty quantification

Model evaluation & data fusion

Comparing process-based methane models and identifying regional biases across North America.

  • Diagnostics across ecoregions
  • Seasonality + interannual variability
  • Scalable workflows on HPC

ML as an accelerator

ML is used as a tool (not a replacement) to speed up expensive components or improve representation where appropriate.

  • Surrogates / emulators
  • Feature selection and regularization
  • Hybrid physics + learning
